The standard flow for bringing a new leader onto the MDplus team. Targeting 30 days from accepted offer to full autonomy.

## Day 0 — Offer accepted

- [ ] Send welcome email with first-week schedule
- [ ] Add to leadership Slack channels (`#leadership-general`, `#leadership-ops`)
- [ ] Add to the leadership Google Group (`leadership@mdplus.community`)
- [ ] Create or invite GitHub account to the org if they&rsquo;ll be touching code

## Week 1 — Orientation

- [ ] 1:1 with Executive Director
- [ ] 1:1 with Chief of Staff
- [ ] Read this entire wiki end-to-end (~45 minutes)
- [ ] Read the [501(c)3 charter](/admin/legal)
- [ ] Shadow one community lead Slack channel

## Week 2 — Embedding

- [ ] Sit in on the leadership weekly sync
- [ ] Pick one ongoing project to co-own
- [ ] Add yourself to the [Leadership roster](/overview/leadership-roster)

## Week 3 — First contribution

- [ ] Publish a Slack intro in `#announcements`
- [ ] Take ownership of an SOP — either write one or refresh one
- [ ] First proposal or memo to the leadership team

## Week 4 — Full autonomy

- [ ] Final check-in with Executive Director
- [ ] Onboarding retrospective — what worked, what didn&rsquo;t
- [ ] Update this page with anything that was unclear

<Callout type="tip" title="Pair them with an onboarding buddy">
Every new leader is assigned a buddy from the existing leadership team. The buddy is the &lsquo;dumb question&rsquo; channel — first stop for anything the new leader doesn&rsquo;t want to ask in a group setting.
</Callout>
