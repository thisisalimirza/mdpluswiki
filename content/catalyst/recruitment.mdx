## Ideal applicant profile

We are recruiting **medical students (MS1–MS4 / M1–M4)** who are motivated, self-starting, and can commit 10–15 hours per week over the summer. The fellowship is designed to be doable alongside a research commitment or other summer activity.

We're screening for "hustle and slope" — someone's trajectory and ability to get things done matters more than where they're starting from.

**Qualities we want to see:**
- Demonstrated initiative (something they've built, organized, or shipped — not necessarily with code)
- Ability to get out and interview users/customers
- Interest in digital health, AI in medicine, or health technology
- Team orientation and ability to collaborate

**Eligibility:**
- Medical students MS1–MS4 at accredited programs
- US-based applicants strongly preferred — fellows on sponsored projects receive stipends which require a US SSN for tax purposes
- International applicants may be considered if they have a US SSN or are truly exceptional; flag these cases during review

## The application

The application is a Google Form with the following components:

- **Short essays:** Two prompts, 350 words total (intentionally brief)
- **Project interest ranking:** Applicants rank sponsored projects by preference; used for matching (note: the ranking logic had a bug with Thalamus's listing — confirm it's fixed before the deadline)
- **Video prompt:** Optional. Intended to differentiate applicants for sponsored/paid tracks
- **Team preference:** Applicants who want to be pre-grouped should list their intended teammates by name. Each person must still submit an individual form. They can use the same essay responses.
- **Audit option:** A sentence in the outreach template clarifies that applicants can join the fellowship in an audit capacity — make sure the updated template is used for large-group sends

## Selection process

The App Review Team (see [Team & Roles](./catalyst-team-roles)) reviews applications during mid–late May 2026.

**Selection principles:**
- Hustle and slope matter more than credentials
- Diversity of background and institution is a goal
- Sponsored tracks require matching applicants to specific company projects — use interest rankings + team composition when making matches
- Shortlisted applicants for sponsored tracks may be further screened via video prompts

**Rubric:** Maya Roytman offered to help build the selection rubric. Confirm this is drafted and shared with the review team before the window opens.

**Team formation:** After individual selections, teams are assembled. ## Sponsor value proposition (for outreach)

When pitching sponsors, use these talking points:

- Access to high-caliber physician innovators — an indirect long-term recruitment pipeline
- Reach across 200+ health systems via the MDPlus network
- Long-term career advocates natively trained in the sponsor's tools and philosophy
- Co-published healthcare case studies as a measurable output
- Clinical expertise is critical to healthcare tech — our fellows are the people who will decide which tools get adopted
