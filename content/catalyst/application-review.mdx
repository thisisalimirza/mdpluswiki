## Scoring rubric

Applications are scored out of **40 points** (50 with optional video bonus). Each reviewer scores independently and provides a verdict.

### Criteria

**Builder Mindset — /20**

What we're looking for: A project they've owned from idea to execution — app, nonprofit, curriculum, prototype, etc.

Application prompt: *"Describe something you've personally built, designed, or significantly improved. Why did you do it? How did you overcome challenges?"*

| Score | Indicator |
|---|---|
| 18–20 | Repeated execution, tangible output, iterative process |
| 14–17 | At least one project with clear ownership |
| 10–13 | Assisted but unclear ownership |
| Under 10 | No demonstrated building |

**Problem Insight — /20**

What we're looking for: Experience-based understanding of a healthcare inefficiency + system-level thinking and proposed action.

Application prompt: *"Think about a healthcare inefficiency or failure you want to fix. Describe the issue and why it's important."* (Essay or 60s video)

| Score | Indicator |
|---|---|
| 18–20 | Experience-based insights with system-level thinking |
| 14–17 | Solid understanding, surface-level response |
| 10–13 | Basic awareness, no offered solution |
| Under 10 | Vague, generic response |

**Video bonus (optional) — /10**

60-second video on a healthcare problem they find interesting. Only score if submitted.

**Total: /40 (+10 bonus)**

### Reviewer verdict

After scoring, each reviewer provides:

1. **Advocacy verdict:**
   - ✅ Yes — strongly advocate
   - ⚠️ Yes with reservations
   - ❌ No

2. **Role archetype** (what role would they play on a team?):
   - 🩺 Clinical Insider
   - 🔧 Builder
   - 📊 Operator
   - 🎯 Strategist

3. **One sentence** on why you'd advocate (or not)

## Review workflow

**Before the window opens:**
- Confirm rubric is finalized and shared with all reviewers
- Divide applications across reviewers (~10–20 applications each)
- Set up a GC (Rishma, Sherman, Emily, Ankith) for cross-referencing team assembly questions

**First pass — Sponsored teams (priority)**
- Match sponsored applicants to company projects using their preference rankings
- Aim for balanced team composition: at least one Clinical Insider + one Builder per team
- 3-person teams preferred for sponsored tracks
- Sherman does a final check on sponsored team assignments

**Second pass — Indie teams**
- Form indie teams from remaining accepted applicants
- 2-person teams acceptable; up to 4 if there's strong cohesion
- Applicants who didn't rank any sponsored project, or who ranked low, go here first

**Audit track**
- Applicants who can't commit 15 hours/week but want access to programming can be admitted as auditors
- The application includes a question about this — don't auto-reject low-availability applicants; consider audit admission

## Team formation

After review is complete, accepted fellows are given access to the [Team Formation Slides](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1Xcf2l6PO5ydk3cfCET13yZ-3tUn7ocjUoLqiLAfLkXk/edit) to self-organize. Each slide has a template for fellows to fill in: name, school/year, skills, what they're working on/looking for, and contact info.

Confirm with Emily Leventhal how edit access was structured for Datathon — whether fellows get direct edit access or submit via a form/request — before replicating for Catalyst.

Pre-formed teams (applicants who listed teammates) should be reviewed together and either accepted as a unit or separated with explanation if there's a quality mismatch.
