## Sponsorship tiers

| Tier | Amount | What they get |
|---|---|---|
| **Community / Startup** | $1,000 | Submit mentors to the fellowship; no project sponsorship |
| **Project Sponsor** | $3,000 | Sponsor one project team; fellows matched to their brief |
| **Lead Sponsor** | $5,000 | Sponsor one project; fellows receive $1K stipend each upon completion |
| **Title Sponsor** | $25,000 | Custom partnership (workshops, keynote, multiple projects, co-branding) |

<Callout type="warning" title="Custom tiers exist">
OpenEvidence negotiated a custom partnership at $15K (between Project Sponsor and Title) that includes a workshop or broader community talk plus two sponsored projects. Don't treat the tier table as rigid — be flexible for high-value partners.
</Callout>

## Value proposition for sponsors

When pitching a company, lead with these points:

- **Talent pipeline:** Direct access to high-caliber physician innovators — a future recruitment pool
- **Network reach:** 5,000+ MDPlus community members across 200+ health systems
- **Long-term advocates:** Fellows become natively trained in the sponsor's tools and philosophy — and are the same people who will influence purchasing decisions in future roles
- **Measurable output:** Co-published healthcare case studies at the end of the fellowship
- **Clinical credibility thesis:** Physicians are critical to building healthcare tech that actually works — and we've seen clinician-informed products succeed where others fail

For title/premium sponsors, also offer: workshop slots with the full cohort, keynote opportunities, and co-branding on the fellowship website and marketing.
